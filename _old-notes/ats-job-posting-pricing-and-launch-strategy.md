# ATS Job Posting Pricing, Verification, Credit, and Launch Strategy

## Overview

This document captures the current commercial, operational, and publication model for the ATS and job posting platform launch. It is intended to serve as an internal reference for product, operations, support, compliance, and marketing teams.

The goal of this model is to:

- create a low-friction path for new employers and recruiters to try the platform;
- preserve a strong trust and legitimacy position in-market;
- reward verified organisations without making the product feel punitive or exploitative;
- prevent abuse through domain-based organisation ownership and credit controls;
- support both direct employers and recruiter organisations;
- provide a clear framework that can be turned into public-facing launch messaging and pricing pages.

This document is intentionally detailed and includes business logic, pricing logic, operational controls, anti-abuse controls, support implications, publication rules, and messaging implications.

---

## Strategic Positioning

The platform is not intended to compete purely on cheap job ads.

Its market differentiation is based on trust, legitimacy, and candidate fairness.

Core positioning pillars include:

- company verification;
- recruiter verification;
- job seeker verification;
- anti-fake-job posture;
- visibility of verification signals before a candidate clicks into a listing;
- support for remote, hybrid, and in-person jobs across all countries;
- broader vertical coverage, not limited to IT;
- a structured platform approach designed to give legitimate employers and candidates a better experience.

The commercial model therefore needs to reflect the following principle:

**Trust is part of the platform standard. Exposure and promotional visibility are separately monetised.**

This is important because the platform should not appear to sell legitimacy as an upsell. Instead, verified status should be visible and meaningful, while paid features focus on visibility, promotion, and workflow enhancement.

## Core Commercial Model

The pricing model has four major layers:

1. **Verification**
2. **Job posting tiers**
3. **Job posting add-ons**
4. **Launch promotional credits**

These layers work together and must be presented clearly.

## Verification Pricing

### Standard Verification
- **A$49**

### Extended Verification
- **A$149**

These verification fees are fixed and are not discounted as part of launch promotions.

### Purpose of Verification
Verification is a trust feature and a public signal. It is not merely an internal administration fee.

Verification supports:

- confirmation that the organisation is legitimate;
- stronger candidate confidence;
- improved click confidence in listings;
- improved trust in recruiter-posted roles;
- differentiation from low-trust job boards;
- stronger marketplace quality over time.

### Badge Visibility
If an employer or recruiter is verified, that verified status is always visible:

- in search results or listing cards;
- on the job listing itself;
- in the organisation presentation wherever applicable.

The verified signal is not hidden behind a premium job tier.

### Badge Types
Verification may be expressed with distinct badge types, such as:

- Verified Employer
- Extended Verified Employer
- Verified Recruiter
- Extended Verified Recruiter

These badge types should be visible, but care should be taken not to make the lower verification tier appear meaningless. Extended Verification should imply broader or deeper checks, not that Standard Verification is weak.

## Verification and Job Posting Bundles

For onboarding and pricing-page simplicity, first-post bundles are offered.

### Standard Verification + First Standard Job Post
- **A$68 total**
- Calculated from A$49 verification + discounted A$19 first standard job post

### Extended Verification + First Premium Job Post
- **A$208 total**
- Calculated from A$149 verification + discounted A$59 first premium job post

### Why These Bundles Exist
These bundles reduce first-purchase friction.

Instead of forcing a new organisation to think in multiple purchase steps, the platform can present:

- a trust purchase;
- plus an immediate first publication path.

This supports conversion and makes the offer easier to understand.

### Bundle Discount Logic
The bundles intentionally provide a small but clear saving compared with buying both components separately.

- Standard Verification + Standard Job Post separately would equal A$78; bundle price is A$68.
- Extended Verification + Premium Job Post separately would equal A$218; bundle price is A$208.

This gives a consistent A$10 effective discount in each case.

## Job Posting Tier Pricing

### Standard Job Post
- **A$29**

### Premium Job Post
- **A$69**

These are the base posting prices outside of the onboarding bundles.

### Standard Job Post
A Standard Job Post is the base listing tier. It is intended to be accessible enough for a new entrant to the market while still preserving quality and perceived value.

### Premium Job Post
A Premium Job Post is a higher-visibility, feature-enhanced listing tier.

It should not be framed as simply “Standard plus a few toggles.” It must be positioned as a distinct visibility and presentation tier.

## Apply Method Handling

A standard listing can handle applications through one of three methods:

- via the platform;
- via email;
- via an external link.

### Pricing Decision
The base posting cost should **not** be split by apply method.

### Rationale
Charging a different price by application method would likely feel arbitrary and would create unnecessary complexity. Application method is a workflow preference, not a core commercial differentiator.

Instead:

- all three application methods should be supported within the same listing tier;
- platform-based application can be encouraged through better analytics and workflow integration rather than forced through pricing;
- email and external link options can be supported with different analytics depth if needed, but not as separate paid tiers.

### Suggested Operational Framing
- **Apply on platform:** best candidate analytics, strongest ATS integration.
- **Apply by email:** available, but less structured analytics.
- **Apply by external link:** available, but may have reduced platform-side insight.

This keeps pricing simple while still allowing the platform to shape preferred behaviour through product design.

## Standard and Premium Feature Structure

### Standard Job Post Includes
A Standard Job Post should include:

- standard listing placement;
- support for applications via platform, email, or external link;
- company logo display;
- standard analytics or standard platform reporting;
- public verified badge display where applicable.

### Premium Job Post Includes
A Premium Job Post should include everything in Standard, plus:

- highlighted listing treatment;
- stronger visibility or ranking treatment in search results;
- highlight colour;
- AI candidate matching;
- video embed;
- public verified badge display where applicable.

### Why Premium Must Be Framed Clearly
The Premium tier is commercially strongest when it is presented as a visibility and presentation tier, not just a bundle of three A$9 add-ons.

If Premium is only described as:

- highlight colour;
- AI candidate matching;
- video embed;

then some buyers may conclude that the premium pricing is simply a convenience bundle.

To avoid that, Premium should also be communicated as having stronger visibility treatment in search and listing presentation.

## Job Posting Add-Ons

Add-ons are deliberately limited and priced modestly at launch to avoid the platform feeling exploitative.

The most important rule is that **Sticky to Top remains a paid feature for everyone**.

It is not included in free launch credits. It is not included automatically in Premium. It remains separately monetised.

## Sticky to Top Pricing

### Verified Organisation Pricing
- **24-hour Sticky to Top:** A$9
- **7-day Sticky to Top:** A$39
- **30-day Sticky to Top:** A$79

### Unverified Organisation Pricing
- **24-hour Sticky to Top:** A$15
- **7-day Sticky to Top:** A$49
- **30-day Sticky to Top:** A$99

### Why Sticky is Kept Paid for Everyone
Sticky is the only feature explicitly excluded from Premium inclusion and credit-based promotions.

This protects:

- a clean monetisation lever during launch;
- a fairness boundary between promotional access and purchased visibility;
- an urgency-based revenue stream;
- the value perception of enhanced placement.

### Why Verified Organisations Receive Better Sticky Pricing
Verified organisations are rewarded with lower Sticky rates because they contribute more trust to the platform and align better with the marketplace quality objectives.

This helps position verification as commercially beneficial without making it compulsory for basic access.

## Standard-Only Add-Ons That Are Included in Premium

The following add-ons are available to Standard Job Posts and are already included in Premium Job Posts:

- **Highlight colour:** A$9
- **AI candidate matching:** A$9
- **Video in job posting:** A$9

### Highlight Colour
Highlight colour is a visual differentiation feature. At launch, the platform supports a single colour highlight but plans to support colour selection.

For Standard listings, highlight colour is an optional paid enhancement.

For Premium listings, highlight colour is included.

### Video in Job Posting
Video supports richer employer branding and better presentation.

This feature allows the embedding of a YouTube or Vimeo video onto the job listing.

For Standard listings, video is a paid add-on.

For Premium listings, video is included.

### AI Candidate Matching
AI candidate matching is a workflow and visibility enhancement feature.

At launch pricing, it is deliberately low-cost at A$9, but it has significantly more value than a purely visual add-on.

This may justify future pricing review or tighter usage controls as the platform matures.

## AI Candidate Matching: Operational Detail

The AI candidate matching feature currently operates conceptually as follows:

- it runs a matching query every 24 hours;
- it detects candidates that match the active job posting;
- it prepares an employer-side summary;
- the summary is sent to the company email address;
- the summary may include ATS rating, resume link, and related matching data;
- only candidates who have enabled the relevant permissions are included;
- candidate notification behaviour may also be supported, subject to final product design and permission handling.

### Minimum Rules That Should Be Stated Internally
The platform should clearly define the initial operational boundary for AI matching:

- the feature runs only for active listings;
- matching only includes candidates who have granted the necessary permissions;
- summaries are sent at defined intervals, currently every 24 hours;
- data shared in summaries must align with candidate privacy settings and platform policy;
- the feature is scoped to the life of the listing unless otherwise stated.

### Candidate Notification Consideration
There is an open design point regarding whether matched candidates should also be notified of suitable jobs.

This should not be publicly over-promised until the workflow is finalised.

For launch messaging, it is safer to frame candidate notification as either:

- permission-based;
- settings-based;
- or under phased rollout.

## Launch Promotional Campaign

### Promotional Offer
Every new company that signs up to the platform receives:

- **10 free Standard Job Post credits**
- valid for the first **90 days** after joining the platform

This is a structured launch trial, not a blanket free-for-all.

### Core Purpose of the Campaign
The campaign is designed to:

- lower adoption friction;
- seed the marketplace with job volume;
- encourage operational trial of the ATS and publication system;
- create a path toward verification conversion;
- preserve monetisation through paid Sticky upsells and verification purchases.

### What the Free Credits Give Access To
The 10 free Standard Job Post credits allow a new organisation to:

- create and publish Standard Job Posts;
- use the platform and test workflow without immediate posting fees;
- experience the core publishing and applicant handling flow.

### Conversion of Unused Credits on Verification
If the organisation chooses to verify its account during the 90-day period:

- any **unused** free Standard Job Post credits are converted into **Premium Job Post credits**;
- this applies whether the organisation completes Standard Verification or Extended Verification;
- the original expiry date remains unchanged.

### Sticky Exclusion Rule
The following rule is absolute:

**Sticky to Top is always a paid add-on and is never included in free credits, converted credits, or Premium by default.**

This rule should be applied consistently in product, pricing pages, support guidance, and marketing material.

---

## Why the Launch Campaign is Strong

The campaign is commercially effective because it creates a clear funnel:

1. sign up;
2. receive meaningful free trial value;
3. experience the platform;
4. see the benefit of verification;
5. convert remaining free inventory into Premium through verification;
6. optionally purchase Sticky for additional exposure.

### Why Free Standard Is Better Than Free Premium
Giving every new organisation 10 free Premium postings from day one would weaken the commercial structure because:

- Premium value would be diluted;
- verification would lose a major upsell benefit;
- paid feature differentiation would become weaker;
- Sticky would become the only clearly monetised launch feature.

By contrast, free Standard credits with Premium conversion on verification maintain a much healthier commercial ladder.

## Credit Rules

Credits are a core operational and commercial control.

They must be governed precisely.

### Credit Type
At launch, the key credit types are:

- Standard Job Post credit
- Premium Job Post credit

### Credit Source
The launch campaign credits should internally be identifiable as promotional credits, for example:

- source: launch promotion
- original type: standard
- converted type: premium, if applicable

### Credit Ownership
Credits belong to the **organisation account**, not:

- the individual user;
- the individual email address;
- the individual domain by itself.

Domains are affiliation and routing signals. The organisation is the commercial container.

### Credit Transferability
Credits are **not transferable**.

This means they cannot be:

- moved between organisations through self-service;
- assigned from one organisation to another by users;
- detached from the organisation that received them;
- re-issued merely because a user changes domain affiliation.

### Credit Expiry
Launch credits:

- expire **90 days from organisation signup**;
- do not reset when converted from Standard to Premium;
- do not extend merely because verification occurs late in the 90-day window.

### Conversion Rule
Only **unused** Standard promotional credits convert to Premium when verification is completed.

Used credits do not retroactively upgrade.

### Credit Consumption Event
The recommended operational rule is:

**A credit is consumed only when a job is approved and published.**

Credits should not be consumed when:

- a draft is created;
- a recruiter submits a draft for approval;
- a listing enters a pending state;
- a listing is edited before publication.

This avoids disputes and aligns billing/credit usage with actual publication value.

## Organisation, Domain, and Identity Model

The platform uses domain-based organisation identity controls.

This is central to platform trust and launch abuse prevention.

### Key Signup Rule
A company or recruiter cannot sign up with a free email account.

The first email account from a domain is used to generate the organisation account.

This applies to:

- company accounts;
- recruiter accounts.

### Why This Rule Exists
This helps prevent:

- throwaway-account abuse;
- trivial promo farming;
- weak organisation identity;
- low-effort fake-company signups.

### Organisation Creation by First Domain Account
Example flow:

- an HR user signs up with `@mrisoftware.com`;
- a company organisation account is created for MRI Software;
- `mrisoftware.com` is attached to that organisation account.

At this point, that organisation becomes the commercial holder of:

- credits;
- listings;
- verification state;
- publishing rights;
- organisation-level settings.

---

## Approved Secondary Domains

The platform already supports approved secondary domains.

These are verified through a DNS TXT record to prove ownership.

### Example Flow
- a user from `@mrisoftware.com` creates the initial organisation account;
- `mrisoftware.com` is assigned to the MRI Software organisation account;
- MRI Software claims ownership of `fsicloud.com`;
- MRI Software verifies `fsicloud.com` by adding a DNS TXT record;
- a user from `@fsicloud.com` later signs up;
- that user is automatically matched to the existing MRI Software organisation account;
- credits remain on the MRI Software organisation account.

### Why This Matters
This supports real-world B2B scenarios such as:

- corporate groups;
- subsidiaries;
- acquired companies;
- regional domains;
- brand-specific domains;
- legacy domains still used by staff.

### Architectural Principle
The correct principle is:

- domains prove organisational affiliation and determine routing;
- the organisation account owns credits, listings, verification, and permissions.

Domains are not separate credit containers.

---

## Domain Ownership Rules

Domain ownership is exclusive and organisation-bound.

### One Domain, One Organisation
A domain can belong to only one organisation at a time.

### No Self-Service Reassignment
Once a domain is claimed to an organisation account, that is authoritative.

Users cannot self-transfer or self-reassign the domain elsewhere.

### No Auto-Merge of Organisations
The platform must **never** auto-merge organisation accounts based on later domain additions or ownership proof.

If a domain is already assigned to an organisation, proving control of that domain elsewhere must not cause an automatic merge.

### Why Auto-Merge Is Prohibited
Silent or automated organisation merges are too risky because they can affect:

- credit ownership;
- billing;
- verification state;
- user access;
- live job listings;
- brand and account ownership;
- recruiter relationships;
- audit history.

### Support-Led Change Requirement
If a domain needs to be moved or reassigned:

- a support ticket must be raised;
- ownership and authority must be verified;
- backend administrative action is required;
- the change must be audited.

This process may involve verification and additional review steps.

### Suggested Internal Rule Statement
**Domain attachment is authoritative and exclusive. Once claimed by an organisation, it cannot be reassigned or used to merge organisations through self-service flows. Any reassignment requires internal review, verification, and manual backend action.**

---

## Recommended Domain Support Workflow

Where a domain conflict or transfer is requested, the internal workflow should include:

1. support ticket creation;
2. ownership and authority evidence collection;
3. review of impacts on users, credits, listings, permissions, and verification state;
4. admin-only backend action if approved;
5. audit logging of the change;
6. notification of affected parties where appropriate.

### Important Credit Implication
Because credits belong to the organisation, a domain transfer should not automatically move credits unless an explicit administrative migration decision is made.

The existence of a domain relationship does not by itself dictate credit reassignment.

---

## User Access and Role Assignment

A verified primary or secondary domain should enable a user to be mapped to the correct organisation account.

However, domain match alone should not automatically grant unrestricted administrative power.

### Recommended Principle
- domain match grants organisation membership or eligibility for membership;
- role-based permissions determine what the user can do inside the organisation.

### Example Permission Areas
Permissions may include:

- publishing or drafting roles;
- recruiter or hiring manager roles;
- approval authority;
- billing administration;
- domain administration;
- verification administration.

This ensures that a user signing up from a valid secondary domain does not automatically gain billing or domain control unless specifically assigned.

## Recruiter Model

Recruiters are a first-class organisation type in the platform and have distinct behaviour.

### Recruiter Signup
Recruiters also cannot sign up with a free email account.

The first email account from a recruiter domain creates the recruiter organisation account.

### Recruiter Verification
Recruiter accounts can also be verified, and verification should be visible publicly in the same general way as company verification.

### Posting Scenarios for Recruiters
Recruiters may post in two broad ways:

1. for their own roles, such as employer-of-record or internal recruitment roles;
2. on behalf of a company.

### Posting on Behalf of a Company
Recruiters can only post on behalf of companies if they verify that they have the right to do so.

This is handled through a **verification token** mechanism.

### Company Approval Requirement
Even if a recruiter prepares or submits the advertisement:

- the company must still approve the job advertisement before it goes live;
- the company approval is the step that authorises publication;
- where company credit is to be used, credit should only be consumed once approval and publication occur.

### Why This Matters
This ensures:

- recruiters cannot burn company credits without approval;
- companies remain in control of their public brand and listings;
- posting rights are permissioned and auditable;
- client-agency relationships are controlled rather than assumed.

---

## Recruiter and Company Credit Usage

The platform needs deterministic rules about which credit pool is used.

### Recommended Logic
- if a recruiter posts a job for its own organisation under its own identity, consume recruiter organisation credit;
- if a recruiter posts on behalf of a company and the role is company-branded and company-approved, consume company credit;
- if the recruiter is acting as employer of record under its own verified organisation identity, consume recruiter organisation credit.

### Credit Control Principle
The organisation that owns the published role and approves publication should be the organisation whose credits are consumed, according to the approved posting flow.

---

## Publication Workflow and Statuses

A structured publication lifecycle should exist to support supportability, audit, and billing/credit accuracy.

### Recommended Status Model
- Draft
- Pending Company Approval
- Approved for Publication
- Published
- Expired
- Rejected
- Withdrawn

### Credit Consumption Rule Within the Workflow
Credit decrement should occur only at **Published**.

This avoids disputes and keeps credit consumption aligned to actual platform delivery.

### Why This Is Important
This supports:

- reliable support handling;
- clear audit trails;
- predictable organisation billing/credit behaviour;
- recruiter and company workflow transparency.

## Publication and Public Display Rules

From a publication perspective, the platform should consistently apply the following logic:

### Verification Display
If the organisation is verified, the verified badge must be visible:

- on the public listing card/search result;
- on the public job detail page.

### Premium Enhancements
If the listing is Premium, it should receive the agreed Premium presentation treatment, which should include both feature inclusion and stronger visibility treatment.

### Sticky Handling
If Sticky is purchased:

- it should be applied according to the purchased duration;
- it should expire according to the selected time window;
- it should remain independent of listing tier and promotional credits.

### Unverified Organisations
Unverified organisations may still be allowed to post, but their pricing is less favourable for Sticky and they will not display the verified trust signal.

This creates a commercial incentive toward verification while preserving a lower-friction funnel.

## Anti-Abuse and Security Controls

The launch campaign is only strong if abuse risk is contained. The platform already includes several strong controls.

### Existing Controls
- no free email account signup for companies or recruiters;
- organisation account creation tied to the first domain signup;
- domain ownership controls;
- approved secondary domains via DNS TXT verification;
- recruiter verification token requirement for posting on behalf of companies;
- company approval requirement before company-branded posts go live;
- organisation-bound credits;
- non-transferable credits;
- 90-day expiry window.

### Why These Controls Matter
These controls reduce the risk of:

- promo farming;
- fake organisations;
- low-effort fake recruitment activity;
- duplicate or fragmented organisation ownership;
- unauthorised recruiter publication;
- invisible use of another organisation’s promotional value.

## Business Logic Summary

The business model now operates on the following logic:

### Verification
Verification is paid and publicly visible. It builds trust and improves conversion confidence.

### Postings
Postings are sold in two tiers: Standard and Premium.

### Add-Ons
Add-ons remain modest, with Sticky reserved as a paid promotional enhancement for all users.

### Launch Credits
Launch credits reduce onboarding friction and increase trial adoption.

### Verification Upsell
Unused launch credits upgrade from Standard to Premium upon verification, reinforcing trust-led conversion.

### Domain and Account Control
Credits are organisation-bound and protected by domain-level ownership controls.

### Recruiter Operations
Recruiters can operate within a structured permission and approval model without bypassing company authority.

## Operational Considerations

The model has several operational implications that should be documented and supported.

### Support Team Needs
Support staff will need clear procedures for:

- domain claim disputes;
- domain transfer requests;
- organisation access issues;
- recruiter authority questions;
- approval workflow confusion;
- credit expiry questions;
- credit conversion questions;
- publication timing disputes.

### Admin Tooling Needs
Backend/admin tooling should support:

- domain attachment and revocation;
- manual domain reassignment where approved;
- organisation audit history;
- credit inspection and credit event history;
- listing publication state history;
- recruiter-company relationship review;
- verification state review.

### Audit Fields Recommended
For each credit event, the platform should ideally record:

- organisation id;
- credit source;
- credit type;
- original credit type if converted;
- created at;
- expires at;
- converted at;
- consumed at;
- consumed by listing id;
- consumed by actor id;
- approved by actor id, where relevant.

For domain actions, it should ideally record:

- domain;
- organisation id;
- action type;
- acting admin id;
- reason;
- evidence or ticket reference;
- timestamp;
- old organisation id and new organisation id where relevant.

## Costing and Revenue Framing

From a costing and revenue perspective, the current design has several strengths.

### Revenue Sources at Launch
- Standard Verification
- Extended Verification
- Standard Job Posts after free credits are exhausted
- Premium Job Posts after free credits are exhausted
- Sticky to Top purchases
- Standard-tier add-ons where selected

### Preserved Revenue During Free Trial Period
Even while organisations receive free posting credits, the platform can still monetise through:

- verification purchases;
- Sticky purchases;
- eventual paid posting once credits expire.

### Why This Matters
A launch campaign should not destroy all early monetisation.

This design preserves revenue while still allowing broad initial trial.

### Why Sticky is Commercially Important
Sticky to Top acts as the most protected paid feature at launch because it:

- is visibility-critical;
- is excluded from free credits;
- is excluded from Premium inclusion;
- supports short-term revenue per listing;
- can be used by both verified and unverified organisations.

### Long-Term Pricing Review Areas
While the current model is launch-ready, the following areas may need future review:

- whether AI candidate matching should remain A$9 or move higher;
- whether Premium visibility should expand further;
- whether volume packs or recruiter bundles should be introduced;
- whether verification validity periods or renewal models are needed;
- whether candidate notification from AI matching becomes a broader platform feature.

## Recommended Internal Policy Statements

The following statements capture the current model accurately and can be adapted into internal docs, support macros, policy notes, and marketing briefs.

### Organisation and Credits
**Credits belong to the organisation account, not an individual user or email domain. Verified primary and secondary domains can be linked to the same organisation, allowing authorised users from those domains to access the shared account and available credits based on their assigned role.**

## Final Assessment

The current model is commercially coherent and operationally defensible.

It balances:

- adoption and trial;
- trust and verification;
- anti-abuse controls;
- publication governance;
- monetisation;
- clear public messaging.

It is a strong launch model because it:

- gives meaningful free access;
- preserves verification value;
- protects premium visibility value through paid Sticky;
- supports recruiter-company workflows;
- uses domain-based account ownership to reduce abuse;
- keeps credits organisation-bound and controlled.

The main remaining work is packaging and presentation:

- pricing page structure;
- FAQ wording;
- onboarding copy;
- marketing narrative;
- internal support and policy documentation.

The underlying logic is strong enough to take to marketing, product, and operations for launch planning.
